About Journal

INFORMAL: Informatics Journal is published by Study Program of Information System, Faculty of Computer Science, Universitas Jember.

INFORMAL: Informatics Journal with e-ISSN 2503-250X publishes current research articles in any area of information system, information technology and informatics. These includes: Decision Support System (DSS), Software Engineering, Information Systems, Geographical Information Systems, AI & Expert Systems, Algorithms & Applications, Machine Learning, Natural Language processing, Neural Networks, Database Systems and Data Mining, Human-Computer Interaction, Multimedia, Image Processing, Pattern Recognition, Computer Networks, Information Security, Parallel & Distributed Systems, Cloud Computing.

INFORMAL: Informatics Journal publishes three issues every year. All papers will be refereed in the standard manner to maintain the article quality.

Open Access Policy: INFORMAL is an open access journal which means that all content is freely available without charge to the user or his/her institution. Users are allowed to read, download, copy, distribute, print, search, or link to the full texts of the articles, or use them for any other lawful purpose, without prior permission from the publisher or the author. This is in accordance with the DOAJ definition of open access.

APC Fee: INFORMAL does not charge any submission fees or article processing charges (APC). All steps of the publication process are free of charge.

Author Guidelines

Submission Requirements

  • Manuscripts should be original and not previously published
  • Minimum length: 5,000 words (excluding references)
  • Maximum length: 10,000 words
  • Format: Microsoft Word or PDF
  • Font: Times New Roman, 12pt, single-spaced
  • Margins: 1 inch on all sides
  • All figures and tables should be numbered and captioned
  • References should follow IEEE citation style
  • Authors must declare the use of AI tools if these are used in their research or writing

Manuscript Structure

  • Title (concise and descriptive)
  • Author names and affiliations
  • Abstract (150-250 words)
  • Keywords (4-6 keywords)
  • Introduction
  • Literature Review
  • Methodology
  • Results and Discussion
  • Conclusion and Future Work
  • References

Peer Review Process

Step 1: Initial Screening

Submitted manuscripts are screened by the editorial team to ensure they meet basic publication standards and fit the journal's scope.

Step 2: Peer Review

Manuscripts that pass initial screening are sent to at least two independent peer reviewers for double-blind review.

Step 3: Editorial Decision

Based on reviewer feedback, the editor makes a decision: Accept, Minor Revisions, Major Revisions, or Reject.

Step 4: Revision and Resubmission

Authors revise their manuscript according to reviewer comments and resubmit with a response letter.

Step 5: Final Decision

The editor reviews the revised manuscript and makes a final acceptance or rejection decision.

Step 6: Publication

Accepted manuscripts are copyedited, proofread, and published in the next available issue.

Publication Ethics and Malpractice Statement

Ethical Standards

INFORMAL is committed to maintaining the highest ethical standards in academic publishing. We follow the guidelines established by the Committee on Publication Ethics (COPE) for all parties involved in the act of publishing: the author, the journal editor, the peer reviewer, and the publisher.

Author Responsibilities

  • Ensure originality and authenticity of work, providing accurate data and referencing.
  • Disclose any conflicts of interest that might influence the results or interpretation of the manuscript.
  • Obtain necessary permissions for data, figures, and materials.
  • Ensure ethical clearance and approval for human or animal research.
  • Properly acknowledge all contributors and ensure co-authors approve the final version.

Editor Responsibilities

  • Maintain strict confidentiality of submitted manuscripts.
  • Ensure a fair, unbiased, and impartial double-blind peer review process.
  • Make editorial decisions solely based on academic merit, relevance, and originality.
  • Address complaints, appeals, and ethical concerns promptly and fairly.

Reviewer Responsibilities

  • Assist in improving the quality of the manuscript through objective feedback.
  • Maintain confidentiality regarding the manuscript.
  • Declare any potential conflicts of interest before agreeing to review.
  • Conduct reviews within the designated timeframe.

Publisher Responsibilities

  • Ensure editorial autonomy and support the editor in dealing with ethical issues.
  • Safeguard the intellectual property and copyright of the journal and authors.
  • Maintain the integrity of the academic record and publish corrections or retractions when necessary.

Use of Generative AI

Authors must explicitly declare the use of generative AI and AI-assisted technologies in the writing or data analysis process. AI tools cannot be listed as an author, as they cannot take responsibility for the submitted work. Authors remain fully accountable for the accuracy, integrity, and originality of their manuscripts, including any AI-generated content.

Copyright Policy

Authors publishing with INFORMAL retain the copyright of their work without any restrictions. The author holds full copyright over their manuscript, maintaining all proprietary rights and granting the journal the right of first publication.

Submission Preparation Checklist

As part of the submission process, authors are required to check off their submission's compliance with all of the following items, and submissions may be returned to authors that do not adhere to these guidelines.

  • The submission has not been previously published, nor is it before another journal for consideration (or an explanation has been provided in Comments to the Editor).
  • The submission file is in OpenOffice, Microsoft Word, RTF, or WordPerfect document file format.
  • Where available, URLs for the references have been provided.
  • The text is single-spaced; uses a 12-point font; employs italics, rather than underlining (except with URL addresses); and all illustrations, figures, and tables are placed within the text at the appropriate points, rather than at the end.
  • The text adheres to the stylistic and bibliographic requirements outlined in the Author Guidelines, which is found in About the Journal.
  • If submitting to a peer-reviewed section of the journal, the instructions in Ensuring a Blind Review have been followed.
